Recognizing and Eliminating Pantry Moths

Pantry moths are a different beast, focusing their attention on dry food products. The most common sign of a pantry moth infestation is discovering small, fluttering moths in kitchen or pantry areas, particularly in the evenings. You might also notice silken webbing within food packages, along the edges of shelves, or in corners. This webbing is produced by the larvae as they feed and move. Finding larvae themselves, which are typically cream-colored with a brown head, in food containers is a sure sign of an infestation. Infested foods may also appear clumped together due to the webbing. Inspecting products like flour, cereal, nuts, dried fruit, pet food, and birdseed is crucial. Even sealed packages can be infiltrated by these persistent pests. Ignoring pantry moths can lead to contaminated food, rendering it unusable and potentially posing health risks if consumed. This involves emptying cabinets and shelves, vacuuming up any visible moths, larvae, pupae, or webbing, and then wiping down surfaces with hot soapy water.
